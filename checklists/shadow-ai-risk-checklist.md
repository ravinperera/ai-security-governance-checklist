# Shadow AI Risk Checklist

Shadow AI is the use of AI tools without formal approval, visibility, or governance.

## Discovery

- [ ] Identify AI tools used by employees.
- [ ] Review browser extension usage where appropriate.
- [ ] Review expense claims for AI subscriptions.
- [ ] Review SaaS usage logs for AI-enabled services.
- [ ] Ask teams which AI tools are used in day-to-day work.
- [ ] Check whether AI meeting bots are joining internal or external calls.

## Risk Indicators

- [ ] Employees paste confidential data into public AI tools.
- [ ] Developers paste proprietary source code into unapproved tools.
- [ ] AI browser extensions have broad permissions.
- [ ] AI meeting assistants record sensitive meetings.
- [ ] Teams use free-tier tools without contracts or data protection terms.
- [ ] AI tools are connected to email, documents, tickets, repositories, or chat without review.

## Controls

- [ ] Publish approved AI tools list.
- [ ] Provide a simple process for requesting new AI tools.
- [ ] Block clearly risky tools where justified.
- [ ] Use SSO and group assignment for approved tools.
- [ ] Train users on what data must not be entered into AI systems.
- [ ] Monitor for unapproved use without creating unnecessary friction.

## Response

- [ ] Confirm what data was entered into the AI tool.
- [ ] Confirm whether the tool stores or trains on entered data.
- [ ] Assess whether customer, personal, or confidential data was exposed.
- [ ] Notify security, privacy, and legal owners where required.
- [ ] Revoke access or approve the tool with controls.
- [ ] Update policy and training if the same issue repeats.
