## Risk assessment

Describe the main risks, including data exposure, prompt injection, insecure output, unauthorised actions, vendor retention, legal or regulatory impact, and operational dependency.

## Compensating controls

List the safeguards that will apply during the exception period. Examples include:

- Limit access to named users or a controlled group.
- Use test, synthetic, anonymised, or redacted data.
- Disable model training or provider retention where contractually available.
- Require human review before outputs are used.
- Restrict integrations, plugins, network access, and automated actions.
- Enable logging and review usage for unexpected activity.
- Store no secrets in prompts, files, logs, or custom instructions.
